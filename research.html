"The Macroeconomic Effect of Fiscal Announcements: Evidence from Spain"

Abstract: This paper examines the short-term impact of fiscal policy announcements and rumors on the Spanish economy, with an emphasis on their informative role. Two high-frequency fiscal sentiment indices—for taxes and public spending—are constructed by manually classifying government announcements (1996–2025) and incorporating them into a BVAR with macroeconomic controls, estimated at quarterly and monthly frequencies using a GDP proxy obtained with a Kalman filter. The results reveal a strong asymmetry: tax announcements immediately reduce GDP without clear effects on revenue, while spending announcements generate persistent expansions and effective increases in spending. These effects are transmitted mainly through the real channel and are amplified when implementation is rapid, highlighting the importance of timely implementation for the effectiveness of fiscal communication.
